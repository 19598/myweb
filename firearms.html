Deer Hunting With Firearms

Deer hunting is extremely popular in ohio. Most people use a shotgun for deer hunting. If you use a shotgun, you can shoot deer with up to a 10 gauge slug. You may not carry more than 3 shells in your shotgun while deer hunting. You must use a slug if you hunt with a shotgun in Ohio. If you choose not to use a shotgun, you can hunt with any straight walled cartridge between .357 and .5 inches wide. Cartridges in this area are mostly for handguns, but if you desire you can purchase a rifle that fires handgun cartridges.

Turkey And Other Bird Hunting With Firearms

If you wish to hunt turkey or other birds, you must use a shotgun. The acceptable shells are 10 gauge and smaller. You should use bird shot, not slugs.

Hunting Coyotes, Feral Hogs, and Small Game

When hunting a coyote, feral hog, or other small game such as a groundhog, any pistol or rifle caliber is allowed. Shotguns allow up to 10 gauge. Keep in mind that if you are hunting an animal to eat it, you don't want to use too big of a caliber and destroy the meat. If you wish, check out my guide to sharpshooting.
